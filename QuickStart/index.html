THE AMERICAN ENTERPRISE PROJECT

What is AEP?

The American Enterprise Project (AEP) is an FBLA chapter event focused on promoting understanding and awareness of the American free enterprise system. Chapters design and implement a project that educates others—such as students, community members, or businesses-about economic principles like entrepreneurship, capitalism, and innovation. Participants document their project in a detailed written report that outlines its objectives, planning, execution, and impact. The project's results are also presented through a formal presentation at FBLA competitions. AEP encourages teamwork, creativity, and leadership by allowing chapters to address real-world economic concepts in an engaging and educational manner. The goal is to inspire greater appreciation for the American enterprise system and its importance in society.

  • Promotes Economic Literacy: The AEP helps individuals understand the principles of the American free enterprise system, fostering informed decision-making and economic awareness.
  • Encourages Entrepreneurship: By highlighting the importance of innovation and competition, the project inspires students to explore entrepreneurial opportunities and contribute to economic growth.
  • Builds Community Awareness: AEP projects bridge the gap between education and the community, emphasizing the value of the free enterprise system in creating jobs, sustaining businesses, and driving progress.
